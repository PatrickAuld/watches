# ChromeTRex

Dive-watch inspired prototype based on the supplied reference image and the later rendered feedback screenshot.

## Source-of-truth note

The later user-provided screenshot is the current visual source of truth for this face. Future iterations should match that screenshot before relying on earlier textual interpretation.

## Intent

Capture the main visual language of the current ChromeTRex direction:
- monochrome steel bezel with a dark outer case
- large white bezel numerals at 50, 40, 30, and 20
- bezel minute hashes concentrated on the right/lower-right arc
- oversized rectangular dial markers with one triangular marker in the upper-right quadrant
- snowflake-style hour hand and broad minute hand
- central seconds hand
- angled `Chrome` / `TRex` text stack in the upper-right quadrant
- small pixel-dino motif above 6 o’clock

## Visual decisions established by feedback screenshot

- The face should read as a **ChromeTRex original homage**, not a literal Tudor reproduction.
- `Chrome` is large and angled; `TRex` sits beneath it in a smaller serif style.
- The dinosaur head silhouette belongs near the upper-right bezel area.
- The pixel dino icon is small and centered below the hands, above 6.
- Bottom text is tiny and split: `PIX…` on the lower-left arc and `…MADE` on the lower-right arc.
- The overall composition is intentionally a little eccentric rather than perfectly conservative.

## Notes

This is still a browser prototype, not a production WFF implementation yet.
The target is the feedback screenshot’s composition and attitude, then later refinement into a format-safe watch-face design.
