# ChromeTRex

Dive-watch inspired prototype based on the supplied reference image.

## Feedback interpretation note

The later user-provided screenshot is **not** the target look. It was shared as an example of a distorted, unorganized result.

Future work should treat that screenshot as evidence of what needs correction, not as the canonical composition.

## Intent

Capture the main visual language of the original desired direction:
- monochrome steel bezel with a dark outer case
- large white bezel numerals with clean, deliberate placement
- coherent bezel minute hashes
- oversized dial markers with a controlled geometric hierarchy
- snowflake-style hour hand and broad minute hand
- central seconds hand
- ChromeTRex-specific branding that feels intentional rather than chaotic
- a dino motif used sparingly and placed with purpose

## Problems revealed by the feedback screenshot

- The overall composition read as distorted and unorganized.
- Decorative elements were too loose and not visually disciplined enough.
- Branding and mascot placement felt more accidental than composed.
- The face drifted away from a clean watch-face layout into a novelty collage.

## Direction for future iterations

- tighten the layout
- reduce accidental eccentricity
- make bezel structure clearer and more balanced
- make marker sizing and spacing more deliberate
- keep the ChromeTRex identity, but integrate it with stronger restraint
- preserve the homage energy without letting the dial become visually messy
- keep branding centered and aligned unless a deliberate offset is requested
- keep the dino motif small and centered above 6 unless a different placement is explicitly requested

## Notes

This is still a browser prototype, not a production WFF implementation yet.
The goal is a cleaner and more intentional ChromeTRex design, informed by the original reference and corrected using the negative feedback from the distorted screenshot.
